Handling Conflict Like a Ninja

This is for beginners

July 20, 2015

Imagine you work with clients. Quite often you have to make promises to them in order to settle their anxiety. The only way to keep those promises is to work hard. Sometimes you have total control of the situation. Often, you have to build relationships and ask for favors. Once in a while something completely out of your control breaks one of your promises and your client is angry.

Imagine we had a software update that wiped out several hours of work from a client with no way to recover it. It was a new client. He didn't enjoy this experience. Even worse all of his trust in our company evaporated.

We ended up resolving the issue with complete honesty and addressing each of his concerns one by one. I've found if you can be respectful while the other person is upset, you earn their respect whether they mention it or not. It can be frightening and crazy-making to withhold judgment while the other person is speaking, but emotional situations typically don't last long. Hopefully, when the dust settles you are closer to solving the problem.
